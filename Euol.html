Eulogy

Early Life

The late Jane Ototo was born in 1958 in Sakwa Kadera (Migori County). She was the second-born daughter of the late Earnest Ogola and the late Deborah Owino. She had seven siblings: the late John Gama, the late Jeremiah Okoth, the late Fridrick, George Owuor, Minoca Anyango, Consolata Mbuya, and Susan Omollo.

Education

Jane began her education at Ranen Primary School and later advanced to Kanyasrega Intermediate School.

Family & Marriage

She was married to the late William Ototo of Agoro West Location as a second wife and co-wife to the late Pamela. She was the loving mother of the late Kennedy, Eunice, and Maurine. Jane was blessed with two daughters: Dorothy and Everline. She was a proud grandmother to eight grandchildren and four co-grandchildren, namely: James, Janeline, Lebrone, Clarence, Fidel, Channel, Chris, Frank, Ashley, Mitchell, Tiffany, Jayden, and the late Willy and Janevive. Her sons-in-law include: John, Benson, and Denish.

Spiritual Life

Jane was initially a devoted member of ACK Church, and later joined The Power of Jesus Ministries, where she remained active until her passing.

Livelihood

Jane was a committed businesswoman operating at Katito Market ("Chiro"), through which she sustained her livelihood and supported her family.

Sickness & Passing

Jane enjoyed good health until 2019 when she developed complications and was later diagnosed with arthritis. She received treatment under the care of the Ogola family in Migori. On April 20th, 2025, at 8:30 AM, she peacefully passed away. May her soul rest in eternal peace.

Abide with me

1. Abide with me; fast falls the eventide;

The darkness deepens; Lord with me abide!

When other helpers fail and comforts flee,

Help of the helpless, O abide with me.

2. Swift to its close ebbs out life's little day;

Earth's joys grow dim; its glories pass away;

Change and decay in all around I see;

O Thou who changest not, abide with me.

3. I need Thy presence every passing hour.

What but Thy grace can foil the tempter's power?

Who, like Thyself, my guide and stay can be?

Through cloud and sunshine, Lord, abide with me.

4. I fear no foe, with Thee at hand to bless;

Ills have no weight, and tears no bitterness.

Where is death's sting? Where, grave, thy victory?

I triumph still, if Thou abide with me!

5. Hold thou thy cross before my closing eyes;

shine through the gloom and point me to the skies.

Heaven's morning breaks, and earth's vain shadows flee;

In life, in death, O Lord, abide with me.
